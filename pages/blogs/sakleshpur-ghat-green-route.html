The Green Route: Sakleshpur to Kukke Subramanya Train Journey

Published on February 22, 2026 • Travel Guide

Deep within the heart of Karnataka's Hassan district lies a stretch of railway that tracks the edge of the world. Known as the Green Route, the train journey from Sakleshpur to Kukke Subramanya is widely considered one of the most stunning and engineering-intensive rail sections in India.

The Western Ghats' Crown Jewel

This 55-kilometer stretch is a part of the Bangalore-Mangalore railway line. As the train descends from the high plateau of Sakleshpur into the coastal plains, it meanders through the dense, untouched forests of the Bisle Ghat. For travelers, it represents a continuous spectacle of nature's untouched corridors, often shrouded in mist and vibrant greenery.

"With a steep gradient of 1-in-50, this section challenge's both locomotive power and human engineering in every meter."

Engineering by the Numbers

Building a railway through the vertical drops of the Sahyadris was a monumental task. The route is a succession of architectural triumphs that leave passengers in awe.

55 km Ghat Section
58 Tunnels
250+ Bridges
1:50 Steep Gradient

As you traverse the route, you'll pass through 58 tunnels totaling over 11 kilometers in length. Between these dark passages lie over 250 bridges, some spanning deep ravines with heights reaching up to 200 meters. The most famous spot, Yedakumeri, offers a panoramic view that has long been a dream for photographers and railway enthusiasts.

Monsoon Magic: Nature in Full Bloom

While beautiful year-round, the Green Route is at its peak during the Monsoon (June–September). The entire valley awakens as hundreds of seasonal waterfalls appear out of thin air, cascading down the rocks. In some tunnels, you can even see water gushing through openings, creating a surreal 'train wash' experience.

The mist often descends into the valleys, making the bridges look like they are floating in the clouds. It is a slow, rhythmic journey where the train rarely exceeds 30 kmph, giving you ample time to soak in the sights.

Traveler Guide: How to Experience It

  • The Daylight Trains: To see the scenery, you must take the daylight trains like the Gommateshwara Express or the Karwar-Bangalore Express. Night trains will leave you in the dark!
  • Window Positioning: The best views are on the right side if you are traveling from Sakleshpur to Mangalore, and on the left side if traveling towards Bangalore.
  • Station Stops: Most express trains stop at Sakleshpur and Subramanya Road. Note that 'Subrahmanya Road' station is about 12km away from the famous Kukke Subramanya Temple.
