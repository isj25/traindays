IRCTC Railway Booking Rules 2026: Traveller-Friendly Guide

Published on February 26, 2026 • IRCTC Rules

Every Indian Railways journey starts with one simple step: understanding the booking rules. In 2026, IRCTC continues to fine-tune reservation policies around advance booking, Tatkal, passenger limits and refunds. This guide breaks down the key rules in plain language so that you can plan your tickets with confidence.

This article is an easy-to-read summary for travellers. Rules can change – always verify details on the official IRCTC and Indian Railways websites before booking.

1. Advance Reservation Period (ARP)

The Advance Reservation Period is the number of days before the journey when booking is allowed.

  • For most long‑distance express trains, ARP is typically around 30–60 days before the date of journey (excluding the day of travel).
  • Premium services, special trains and some short‑distance trains may follow a different ARP notified by Indian Railways.
  • Tickets can be booked both online via IRCTC and at PRS counters during this window.

*Exact ARP can vary by train category and notification. Check current rules on Indian Railways/IRCTC before booking.

2. General Quota Booking Basics

Most passengers book under the General Quota, which follows these broad rules as per IRCTC terms and conditions:

  • You can book up to 6 passengers per PNR in a single general‑quota ticket.
  • Names, age and gender of all passengers must match the ID card produced during the journey.
  • Concessions (for example, senior citizen concession) are available in general quota as per Railway rules and must be selected while booking.
  • Booking is allowed both online (IRCTC) and offline (reservation counters) within the applicable ARP.

3. Tatkal Booking Window & Timings

The Tatkal scheme is meant for last‑minute confirmed travel and has a much shorter booking window:

  • Tatkal tickets open one day before the date of journey (excluding the day of journey).
  • As per current rules, typical opening times are:
    • 10:00 AM for AC classes (1A, 2A, 3A, CC, EC).
    • 11:00 AM for non‑AC classes (SL, 2S).
  • Only up to 4 passengers per PNR are allowed under Tatkal.
  • No concessions (including senior citizen discounts) are permitted on Tatkal tickets.

Tatkal Charges Snapshot

In addition to the base fare, Tatkal attracts an extra charge which varies by class and distance. Typical ranges reported in 2026 traveller guides are:

  • Sleeper (SL): around ₹100–₹200
  • AC 3‑Tier (3A): around ₹300–₹400
  • AC 2‑Tier (2A): around ₹400–₹500
  • Chair Car (CC) / Executive (EC): distance‑based, generally in a similar band

The official rule framework also mentions that Tatkal is usually calculated as 10% of basic fare for Second Class and 30% for other classes, subject to minimum and maximum limits notified by Railways.

4. ID Proof and Passenger Verification

  • For online bookings, at least one passenger must carry original photo ID during the journey.
  • Accepted IDs include Aadhaar, passport, voter ID, driving licence, PAN card and other government‑issued documents as listed by IRCTC.
  • Details entered at the time of booking (name, age, gender) should match the ID to avoid penalties or being treated as travelling without a valid ticket.

5. Cancellation & Refund Overview

Refund rules differ for general and Tatkal tickets. The high‑level picture from IRCTC rule summaries:

  • Confirmed general tickets attract graded cancellation charges depending on how many hours before scheduled departure you cancel (more refund if cancelled earlier).
  • Waitlisted and RAC tickets are generally refunded after deducting a nominal clerkage fee if cancelled before chart preparation.
  • Confirmed Tatkal tickets are normally non‑refundable if cancelled by the passenger, except in special cases such as:
    • train cancellation by Railways,
    • train diverted and passenger not travelling,
    • coach or AC failure with downgraded accommodation,
    • passenger made to travel in a lower class.

In such exceptional scenarios, base fare may be refunded as per Railway rules, but Tatkal charges themselves are generally not refunded.

6. Practical Tips to Stay Within the Rules

  • Always confirm the latest ARP and Tatkal timings for your specific train on IRCTC or the official Railway websites.
  • Avoid multiple logins of the same IRCTC user ID during Tatkal opening minutes—this can lead to blocks or failed payments.
  • Fill passenger details and ID information before the booking window opens so that you only need to confirm and pay when the clock hits.
  • For family travel with concessions (e.g. seniors), explore general quota well in advance instead of relying only on Tatkal.
