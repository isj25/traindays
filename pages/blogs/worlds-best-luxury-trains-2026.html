World’s Best Luxury Trains to Experience in 2026

Published on February 26, 2026 • Global Travel Guide

For many railfans, the ultimate dream is not just reaching a destination but living inside a moving five‑star hotel. Around the world, a handful of legendary trains combine heritage coaches, fine‑dining and cinematic landscapes into once‑in‑a‑lifetime journeys. Based on 2026 travel round‑ups and luxury rail guides, here are some of the most talked‑about luxury trains you can aspire to ride.

Maharajas’ Express (India)

Frequently described as one of the world’s leading luxury trains, the Maharajas’ Express runs multi‑day circuits across North and Central India. Suites are styled like royal residences, and curated off‑train excursions cover forts, palaces and wildlife parks.

  • Journey length: typically 3–7 nights, with themes such as “Heritage of India” and “Treasures of India”.
  • Experience: all‑inclusive fine dining, bar cars, butler service and guided sightseeing.
  • Price band: from upper mid four‑figures to well above USD 25,000 per cabin on some departures, according to luxury‑train tour operators.

Venice Simplon‑Orient‑Express (Europe)

Operated by Belmond, this train keeps alive the spirit of 1920s Art Deco glamour. Polished wood panelling, vintage compartments and black‑tie dinners make it one of the most photographed trains in the world. In 2026, travel features highlight new routes such as Paris to the Amalfi Coast including exclusive excursions to Pompeii.

  • Classic route: London/Paris to Venice, with seasonal journeys across Europe.
  • Cabin types: historic cabins, suites and ultra‑luxurious Grand Suites.
  • Indicative price: roughly from £3,500 per person for shorter journeys, rising to five‑figure fares in Grand Suites, as per 2026 European luxury‑travel articles.

Belmond Royal Scotsman (Scotland)

The Royal Scotsman is often described as a country house on wheels. It tours the Scottish Highlands with tweed interiors, open‑air observation decks and small guest capacity, keeping the atmosphere intimate and club‑like. 2026 coverage notes collaborations with Michelin‑starred chefs and new Grand Suites.

  • Routes: short 2–7 night itineraries through castles, lochs and whisky distilleries.
  • Highlights: spa car, fine Scotch tastings and guided country excursions.
  • Price band: selected Grand Suite experiences are quoted at £12,000+ per person for two‑night trips in 2026 travel reports.

The Ghan (Australia)

The Ghan cuts across the Australian continent, linking Adelaide, Alice Springs and Darwin. It is less about chandeliers and more about wide‑screen outback panoramas combined with premium comfort. In 2026, operators are introducing upgraded luxury suites with more space and private bars.

  • Signature journey: 3–4 day north–south crossing through the Red Centre.
  • 2026 update: new “Australis” and “Aurora” suites reportedly offer around three times the space of standard cabins.
  • Experience: off‑train experiences in deserts, gorges and remote towns, paired with regional Australian cuisine.

Seven Stars in Kyushu (Japan)

Often labelled the world’s most exclusive luxury train, Seven Stars in Kyushu operates limited departures on the Japanese island of Kyushu. With very few suites and a lottery‑based booking system, it is incredibly hard to get a seat.

  • Booking: many 2026 articles mention that applications are accepted only during set periods, followed by a draw.
  • Focus: volcanic landscapes, hot springs, traditional ryokan‑style hospitality and seasonal Japanese cuisine.
  • Design: handcrafted interiors with local wood, textiles and art rather than over‑the‑top opulence.
