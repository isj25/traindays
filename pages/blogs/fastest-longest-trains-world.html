Speed & Scale: World's Fastest and Longest Trains

Published on February 26, 2026 • Railway Facts

In the world of railways, two metrics reign supreme: Speed and Scale. While some engineers focus on shaving seconds off commute times with magnetic levitation, others are building massive freight convoys that stretch for miles. Let's explore the absolute titans of the tracks in 2025.

The Kings of Speed

High-speed rail is no longer just about getting from point A to B; it's a demonstration of aerodynamic and electronic mastery. China currently dominates the operational scene, while Japan holds the absolute laboratory records.

603 km/h

JR Central L0 Series Maglev (Japan)

The absolute world speed record for a train, achieved during testing in 2015. Commercial operations are planned for 2027.

460 km/h

Shanghai Maglev (China)

The world's fastest operationally commercial train. It connects Pudong Airport to the city center using magnetic levitation.

The Titans of Scale

While passenger trains compete for speed, freight trains compete for sheer volume. Moving tens of thousands of tonnes across continents requires immense power and length.

4,088m

Shuozhou-Huanghua Freight (China)

A 4.1km long train with 324 carriages and four electric locomotives, carrying over 32,400 tonnes of coal.

9,289km

Trans-Siberian Railway (Russia)

The world's longest continuous train route, spanning 8 time zones from Moscow to Vladivostok.

In the United States, Union Pacific operates intermodal trains that frequently exceed 3 miles (4.8 km) in length, showcasing the incredible efficiency of rail for moving goods across the vast American landscape.
