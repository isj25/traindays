Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Indian Railways booking rules

What is the IRCTC 60-Days Advance Booking Rule?

The Advance Reservation Period (ARP) allows you to book train tickets up to 60 days before your travel date (excluding the day of the journey). This rule became effective on November 1, 2024, replacing the previous 120-day window.

What time does IRCTC booking open daily?

  • General Quota: Opens at 8:00 AM IST daily.
  • AC Tatkal (1A, 2A, 3A, CC): Opens at 10:00 AM IST, one day before travel.
  • Sleeper Tatkal (SL): Opens at 11:00 AM IST, one day before travel.

Does the 60-day rule apply to all trains?

Most long-distance trains follow the 60-day rule. However, some short-distance, intercity, and special festival trains may have different booking periods (like 30 days or less). Always check specific train details on the IRCTC website.

What is the new Aadhaar verification rule?

From January 12, 2026, only Aadhaar-verified users can book general reserved tickets on the very first day the booking window opens (ARP day). Non-verified users can book from the second day onwards, subject to availability.

Is Tatkal booking affected by the 60-day rule?

No, Tatkal booking remains a last-minute service. It opens precisely one day before the train's departure from its originating station at 10:00 AM for AC and 11:00 AM for Sleeper classes. Check out our Pro Tactics for Tatkal booking to increase your chances of getting a confirmed ticket.

What happens if my travel date is more than 60 days away?

If your travel date is more than 60 days away, you cannot book yet. Use our calculator to find the exact date and time your booking window will open, and set a reminder!
